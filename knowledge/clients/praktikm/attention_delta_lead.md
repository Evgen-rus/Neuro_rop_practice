# Lead pack: qualification and safe processing

1. A meaningful contact is a verified substantive conversation with the client. A busy line, no answer, voicemail, an unavailable subscriber, a technical error or an internal comment is not meaningful contact.
2. Qualification reflects confirmed need, timing, budget or budget position, decision-maker path and relevant technical parameters. Unknown facts stay unknown; do not repeat a full first qualification when a substantive contact has already established part of it.
3. Distinguish lead quality from processing quality. A weak or missing CRM follow-up can be bad processing even when the lead is not proven bad. Do not label a lead non-target solely because a phone attempt failed or context is partial.
4. For no-contact handling, use the factual outcome of the latest reliable call attempt. A precise busy or invalid-number outcome has priority over generic no-contact recovery. Do not infer invalid number from busy, unavailable, voicemail or no answer.
5. Move a lead to a deal only when need, timing, a practical next step and the necessary commercial/technical basis are confirmed. Do not invent budget, a sent proposal or an agreed date to justify the move.
6. After a meaningful but incomplete contact, preserve the established need and collect only the missing qualification or technical facts with a dated follow-up. Record the result in CRM.
