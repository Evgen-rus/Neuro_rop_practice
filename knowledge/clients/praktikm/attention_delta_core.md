# Core: safety of the management decision

Use this pack with the compact attention decision, not as a source of CRM facts.

1. Treat only CRM history, the supplied transcript and CRM stage policy as facts about a client. Knowledge rules explain how to evaluate those facts; they do not prove them.
2. Reference only visible, stable evidence IDs. Missing data, a missing transcript, a CRM stage, an invoice or an internal note do not by themselves prove a client promise, refusal, payment intention, budget, competitor or lack of need.
3. State an uncertainty as a data gap. Do not turn it into a loss, an urgent escalation, a repeated qualification or a client failure without evidence.
4. A useful management action has an owner, a concrete check, an internal control deadline and an expected CRM fact. An internal deadline is never a client commitment or a client delay.
5. Do not close, return to pipeline, move to a deal or start a deeper commercial/engineering step without the supporting facts required for that decision. Preserve an active sale when the evidence supports it.
6. Separate a confirmed event from a proposed next step. The CRM fact after contact must record what actually happened and what follows from that outcome.
