# Deal pack: control of an active commercial decision

1. CRM stage is context, not proof. Distinguish an open sale, a confirmed closure, a disputed closure and insufficient evidence. Do not reopen or close a deal automatically.
2. A sent invoice is not payment intent. Keep separate: invoice sent, invoice agreed, contract signed, advance agreed, payment date confirmed and payment received. Do not turn any missing value into a payment promise.
3. If price or competitor comparison is evidenced, control the actual commercial uncertainty: what is compared, price or terms gap, budget or confirmed non-disclosure, decision-maker, decision date and whether the invoice is an agreed next step. Do not prescribe a repeat presentation without a reason.
4. A missing technical input may block a calculation or proposal while the sale remains active. Request the concrete missing data and a client date for providing it; do not treat an internal control deadline as a client promise or start deep engineering work before required inputs arrive.
5. For a disputed closed deal, request a documented review of closure reason, client need, commercial context and contradiction between history and CRM state. Reopening requires a ROP decision, not an automatic action.
6. Every deal control action must preserve the responsible manager, an internal deadline, the next CRM task and the expected CRM fact. A concrete next step is preferable to a generic instruction to monitor the deal.
